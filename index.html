Steam: Stink or Steal Tallowmere (Review)

Nicholas Davison
March 30, 2015

Why hello there everyone, I'm Adam The How to Gamer and welcome to Steam: Stink or Steal. The show where I review currently on sale games in two minutes or less(not including intro and outro) every Saturday, and sometimes during the week. This week's episode, I'll be taking a look at Tallowmere, and letting you know-with the current sale- whether it's worth your hard earned cash, or just a steaming pile of shit. Here we go...

About the game

Tallowmere is a 2d adventure platformer released on steam march 3rd. It is currently on sale for $4.49 USD, ten percent off its $5 price tag. It's compatible with Windows Mac and Linux on fairly low caliber systems and is only 200Mb so it should download in no time. It's useable with keyboard and mouse and has Xbox 360 and PS3 controller support, though button-mapping may be required.

Gameplay

The basic gameplay largely consists of dungeon exploration and killing enemies you encounter in each room to increase your character's power and progress to the next room. One such way your power increases is through the interesting but limited loot system.  Blowing stuff up in a medievalesque game with items like the grenade can be a lot of fun, but that being said Tallowmere is genuinely challenging, despite only having two combat mechanics: attack and block. There are real strategy elements that prevent you from just trying to hack and slash, or blow up your way through levels. It also looks like you can hook up some controllers to play with your friends locally, but I didn't get a chance to try that out.

Major Faults

Tallowmere has minimal story and no obvious backstory to help us understand why we must trek through these perilous dungeons. An element I found personally frustrating, is all your progress is lost when you die. While this might be fun as an extra mode it significantly lessens the games replayability. I think even adding a revive token purchasable from the vender would be a decent fix. It would still prevent saves, but allow players that have progressed far enough and earned enough gold to get a second chance on particularly challenging rooms. It should be noted that I have not encountered any bugs or glitches at this point, which is how it should be in such a small game.

Stink or Steal?

Tallowmere is a steal at its current price because it offers enough versatility with its simplistic gameplay elements that you can play a quick fifteen to twenty minutes and see how far you get. It should also be noted that if you exit the game your progress is saved in the last room you entered, and is only erased when you die. The developer has also stated on their website that they will continue to update and improve their game in the future, which is always a good sign. Most of all the game is genuinely fun and challenging, making it a great addition to your steam library.

Consensus: Steal

Spotify Launches on PS4 and PS3

Nicholas Davison
March 30, 2015

The widely used music service Spotify has just been launched on all PS4 and PS3 consoles. The company has closed down its in-house music service and has signed a contract with one of today's biggest players in the music-streaming industry. Just like Spotify's regular service, users can enjoy a free ad-supported version of the player, or can pay up to $10 a month for ad-free music. An Xbox One version is currently not in the works, although you can go to Spotify's forums and add to their ongoing discussion.
